I am a research associate at the University of Cambridge working with Prof. Cecilia Mascolo. My work enables efficient and reliable ML and AI to make the most out of real-world data (e.g. timeseries, audio, images, radio, or other modalities) on mobile and IoT/edge devices for Mobile Sensing and Applications. I am particularly interested in the following mobile/wearable paradigms:

  • Mobile computing/sensing systems. Mobility, interactivity, context awareness or security -- What are their limits?
  • On device learning. Resource constraints and low latency on wearables -- How to train and inference models efficiently?
  • Model adaptation and generalization. Signal changes in real-world environments -- How to ensure the trained model is robust?

Previously, I completed a PhD in Computer Science at the The University of New South Wales working with Prof. Wen Hu. I am a recipient of Australian Government Research Training Program (RTP) Scholar (2017-2021) and ACM MobiSys Rising Star (2023).
